Cambridge Neurotech probes, Phy, and Intan

Probes made by Tim Harris at Janelia and distributed by Cambridge Neurotech are great. Here is a gif of them going into the brain. Compare that to a another probe which shall not be named. It distort thse brain surface before shooting in. The spike sorting is a bit trickier than with tetrodes, but the result is worth the trouble: I had a hard time getting the mapping right, so here are a few notes and scripts I generated in the process. Hope they help someone. Here is a little hand-drawn map courtesy of Ekaterina Morozova. Depicted is a 64-channel probe with two Omnetics connectors. We connect it to a 64-channel Intan headstage with an Open Ephys system. The numbers on shanks will likely be different in your case. here are some Matlab scripts that go from .continuous files recorded in Open Ephys to .dat files that the spikesorting package Phy can understand. This function does everyting: oe_run_phy_one_shank(path_to_data, folder_experiment_id, folder_to_write,shanks)
